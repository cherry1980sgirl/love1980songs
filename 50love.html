TOP 50 LOVE SONGS OF THE 1980'S

From a decade known for neon lights, obtuse fashion, and romantic coms, comes fifty of the most beautiful love songs ever written!

#50 "Don't You (Forget About Me)" SIMPLE MINDS 'The Breakfast Club Soundtrack' [1985]


Don't You (Forget About Me) captures the challenges of having friendships outside of social circles. It is a natural pairing for "The Breakfast Club" which explores the interactions and commonalities of five teenagers from different high school cliques during their life-changing Saturday school detention.

"Brian Johnson: Dear Mr. Vernon, we accept the fact that we had to sacrifice a whole Saturday in detention for whatever it was we did wrong. But we think you're crazy to make us write an essay telling you who we think we are. You see us as you want to see us - in the simplest terms, in the most convenient definitions. But what we found out is that each one of us is a brain...
Andrew Clark: ...and an athlete...
Allison Reynolds: ...and a basket case...
Claire Standish: ...a princess...
John Bender: ...and a criminal.
Brian Johnson: Does that answer your question? Sincerely yours, the Breakfast Club."

#49 "I Can Dream About You" DAN HARTMAN 'The Streets Of Fire Soundtrack' [1984]


I Can Dream About You was performed by The Sorels, a music group created specifically for the film. The music and lyrics were composed by Dan Hartman, a disco artist who was famous during the late 1970's.

Streets of Fire is the action packed neo-noir musical rock 'n roll adventure that begins when Ellen Aim, a beautiful rock music star is kidnapped by bikers while singing in the spotlight wearing a red dress.
Tom Cody, her ex-boyfriend, and a small group go to free her.
According to director Walter Hill, the film's origins came out of a desire to make what he thought was a perfect film when he was a teenager and put in all of the things that he thought were "great then and which I still have great affection for: custom cars, kissing in the rain, neon, trains in the night, high-speed pursuit, rumbles, rock stars, motorcycles, jokes in tough situations, leather jackets and questions of honour".

Originally composed by Michael Masser (center) and Gerry Goffin (right), "Nothing's Gonna Change My Love for You" was first recorded in 1984 by legendary jazz singer and guitarist George Benson (left) for his 1985 studio album 20/20.

#46 "If Ever You're in My Arms Again" PEABO BRYSON 'Straight from the Heart' [1984]



Written by Cynthia Weil, Tom Snow, and Mike Masser.

A song about lost love and deep regrets, "If Ever You're in My Arms Again" is sung by thoughtful man, looking back on the past and having regrets about the way he treated his lover. After separating he realizes how badly he took advantage of her and how much she had done for him, and would do anything for a second chance.

#45 "Let's Hear If For The Boy" DENIECE WILLIAMS 'The Footloose Soundtrack' [1984]


Star of soul, Deniece Williams, begain her career as a gospel singer in her church choir. After being introduced to Stevie Wonder by her cousin, she joined his trio of backup singers, and toured with the band for several years while raising her two sons.

Footloose is a dance/musical love story about a teenager from Chicago and the wild daughter of a small town minister. The movie has inspirational roots in places like Elmore City, Oklahoma, a town known for its strict anti-dance ordinance in effect from 1898-1980.
